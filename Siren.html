Disaster First Aid Management

Learn essential first aid techniques and preparedness strategies to manage emergencies effectively.

Disaster-Specific Preparedness and First Aid
🌍 Earthquake
✓Secure furniture, heavy objects, and appliances.
✓Identify safe spots (under sturdy tables, doorways).
✓Keep an emergency kit ready (water, food, flashlight).
✓Indoors: Drop, Cover, and Hold under a sturdy object. Stay away from windows.
✓Outdoors: Move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and power lines.
✓In a Vehicle: Stop safely away from bridges and buildings. Stay inside.
✓Expect aftershocks; move to an open, safe location.
✓Check for injuries and apply first aid.
✓Inspect gas, water, and electrical lines for damage.
✓Avoid using elevators and unstable structures.
🌊 Flood
✓Know your evacuation route and nearest shelter location.
✓Store emergency supplies on upper floors.
✓Keep important documents in waterproof containers.
✓Evacuate immediately when instructed. Move to high ground.
✓Never walk, swim, or drive through flood waters.
✓If trapped, go to the highest level — only to the roof if necessary.
✓Avoid contact with floodwater — it may be contaminated.
✓Boil or purify water before drinking.
✓Document damage for insurance and relief assistance.
🌀 Cyclone
✓Follow cyclone warnings from IMD. Board up windows and doors.
✓Store 3–7 days of food, water and emergency supplies.
✓Stay indoors in the strongest part of the building.
✓The eye of the storm may cause brief calm — do not go outside.
✓Watch for downed power lines and structural damage.
✓Use generators outdoors only to avoid CO poisoning.
🔥 Fire Emergency
✓Install smoke detectors on every level of your home.
✓Plan and practice two escape routes from every room.
✓RACE: Rescue, Alert, Confine, Extinguish/Evacuate.
✓Stay low under smoke. Feel doors before opening — if hot, find another exit.
✓Call 101 immediately.
✓For burns: cool with running cold water for 10 minutes.
✓Do not re-enter building until fire department clears it.
